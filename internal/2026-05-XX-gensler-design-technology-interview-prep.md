## Part 5 — The three questions to ask Islay

These are calibrated to her actual public writing and Gensler's platform identity. Each one signals you've done the homework without sounding scripted.

1. **"How is the NE region's design-tech team scoped against NFORM and gFloorz 2.0 — do regional DT directors consume the global toolchain, or extend it with regional contributions back to the platform?"**
   - *Why this works*: Signals you've read Joseph Joseph's NFORM strategy and understand platform-vs-region tension. Lets Islay describe her actual leverage and budget.

2. **"Your IoT-+-BIM digital-twin essay framed BIM as a live post-occupancy data substrate. Where is the team on closing that loop today — is sensor ingestion happening on real projects, or still pilot phase?"**
   - *Why this works*: Direct callback to her own published work. Shows you read her writing and respect it. Surfaces whether the role would be greenfield R&D or applied to live jobs — that determines what level you should be slotted at.

3. **"How does the Northeast DT team divide work between Morristown and NY — is the Morristown role primarily client-facing on NJ life-sciences/workplace projects, while NY owns the cross-region computational stack?"**
   - *Why this works*: Lets you learn the seat geometry without seeming to negotiate office location, and signals you understand the Eric-Bieber-tier office-level DT vs. Islay-tier regional DT distinction.

## Part 6 — Two things to avoid saying cold

1. **DO NOT pitch "we should build our own NFORM-equivalent."** Gensler has invested years and budget in NFORM / gFloorz / WPI / EXI as their proprietary moat. Joseph Joseph (CDO) personally unveiled gFloorz 2.0. Pitching to replace it positions you as not having done your homework. **Frame all contributions as extensions to NFORM, not alternatives.**

2. **DO NOT be dismissive of AI in architecture, or pitch it as hype.** Aubin Gastineau is teaching public AI courses; the Design Forecast 2026 press release leads with AI; Vignesh Kaushik publishes on AI/ML at conferences. **Gensler's public position is bullish-AI.** If you have private skepticism about LLMs in design workflows, phrase it as a *"how is the firm hardening AI outputs against hallucination on regulated code/spec documents"* — a craftsman's question, not a contrarian's.
